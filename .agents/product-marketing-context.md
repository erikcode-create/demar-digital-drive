# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-03-29*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** DeMar Transportation is an asset-based freight carrier and licensed broker providing nationwide shipping and logistics solutions.
**What it does:** Moves freight across the US using our own fleet of trucks (dry van, reefer, flatbed, box truck, sprinter van, hazmat-certified) and an extended carrier network. Also provides 3PL logistics coordination and warehousing through partner facilities.
**Product category:** Freight transportation and logistics
**Product type:** Service (B2B)
**Business model:** Per-load pricing (spot and contract), 3PL management fees, warehousing rates

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Small-to-mid-size manufacturers, distributors, e-commerce companies, and retailers that ship freight regularly. Also agricultural producers, construction companies, and any business needing temperature-controlled, flatbed, or hazmat shipping.
**Decision-makers:** Logistics managers, supply chain directors, operations managers, business owners, procurement teams
**Primary use case:** Reliable, competitively priced freight transportation with a single point of contact
**Jobs to be done:**
- Move freight on time without damage
- Find available capacity quickly (especially during tight markets)
- Simplify logistics with one provider that handles multiple equipment types
**Use cases:**
- Full truckload (FTL) shipments nationwide
- Less-than-truckload (LTL) consolidated shipping
- Temperature-controlled pharmaceutical/food shipments (reefer)
- Oversized or heavy equipment moves (flatbed)
- Last-mile and expedited delivery (sprinter van, box truck)
- Hazmat-certified freight
- 3PL logistics management for companies without in-house logistics teams
- Warehousing and distribution through partner facilities

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Shippers struggle to find reliable, fairly priced freight capacity — especially across multiple equipment types — without managing a patchwork of carriers.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Pure brokers lack asset control — no guarantee of service quality
- Pure asset carriers lack capacity flexibility — can't cover surges or specialized equipment
- Mega-carriers treat small/mid shippers as low priority
**What it costs them:** Late deliveries damage customer relationships. Capacity gaps cause production delays. Managing multiple carriers wastes time and increases error rates.
**Emotional tension:** Fear of a load not showing up. Frustration with unreturned calls. Stress of not knowing where freight is.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Regional and national trucking companies (e.g., Schneider, Werner, JB Hunt) — falls short because SMB shippers get deprioritized; rigid service offerings
**Secondary:** Pure freight brokers (e.g., CH Robinson, TQL, Coyote) — falls short because no asset control means inconsistent service quality; driver relationships are transactional
**Indirect:** In-house fleets — falls short because capital-intensive, hard to scale, and limited to one equipment type

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Dual authority: motor carrier (MC) + freight broker — own trucks AND access to carrier network
- Full equipment range under one roof (dry van, reefer, flatbed, box truck, sprinter, hazmat)
- 3PL and warehousing capabilities through partner network
- 24/7 availability with US-based, English-speaking team
- Safety-first culture with DOT compliance and excellent safety ratings
**How we do it differently:** We combine the reliability of an asset-based carrier with the capacity flexibility of a broker. One call connects you to our fleet AND our network.
**Why that's better:** More capacity options, consistent service quality on our own trucks, and broker-network backup during surges — all through a single point of contact.
**Why customers choose us:** Transparency, competitive rates, responsive communication, and the peace of mind that comes from working with a company that has skin in the game (our own trucks on the road).

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Unreliable current carrier (missed pickups, damaged freight, poor communication). Broker not returning calls during tight markets. Paying premium rates without premium service.
**Pull:** Asset-based reliability + broker flexibility. One provider for all equipment types. Responsive, US-based team. Competitive rates with transparent pricing.
**Habit:** "We've always used [current carrier/broker]." Existing EDI integrations. Familiarity with current sales rep.
**Anxiety:** "Will the transition be smooth?" "Can they really handle our volume?" "What if their fleet isn't big enough?"
